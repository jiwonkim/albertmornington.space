Tailing Albert to Brighton:
a tale of sore butts, lust, and 4 flat tires

The last time I had ridden my beautiful fixie before the Brighton weekend was in 2013, when I was briefly experimenting with a bicycle-train-walk hybrid commute from Mountain View to San Francisco. One evening I got off the train after a long day, unlocked my bike from the rack, and hopped on. I realized the brakes were no longer working at (or rather, while speeding past) the first stop sign. Further inspection revealed that someone had intentionally cut my brake cables. No matter how much I puzzled over it, I couldn’t think of a way for a perfectly solid metal cable to accidentally end up with a surgical incision.

From 2013 to 2015, I let my then-boyfriend-and-roommate use my bike, since he had no qualms riding a bike without brakes (he argued that it did have brakes, it was a fixie after all - you just had to fight the pedals to slow down). Besides, his workplace was 0.9 miles from home (just enough to qualify for the living-close-to-work rent subsidy) so he wouldn’t be covering much distance anyway.

When I moved to London, my company offered to ship a big shipping-container-full of stuff to my new place. I fitted my bike with a rear brake, flipped over the rear wheel so it was no longer a fixie, and sent it over. But from October 2015 to February 2016 it lay neglected in my back yard until Albert came over to fix it up for our big trip. We (read: he) fitted front and back fenders and plumped my wheels while we (read: I) watched three epidoes of House of Cards projected onto the wall. Albert was most concerned about how my single-speed bike would climb hills we would encounter, if any. I was most concerned about being violated by the bike seat and tacked on a gel seatpad.

We woke up early on Saturday morning and headed out. I had never biked through London traffic before, and throughout the turns and twists and signals and filtering, I kept my eyes glued to Albert’s butt and tailed him very closely behind.

When we were out of London, we strapped a bluetooth speaker to the back of Albert’s bike and connected it to my phone. This bluetooth link turned into an invisible leash that would let Albert know when I was falling behind, because the music would start breaking up. (Mildly interesting: it only worked when Albert was in front, because if I were in front instead, my body would swallow the bluetooth signal and block it entirely. Turns out, outside of the city, without the cars and buildings for the signal to bounce off of, you need to keep the space between the device and speaker clear of any signal-absorbing things).

Muddy, unpaved roads were my biggest enemy. I’d only ever ridden my bike on paved and gloriously straight Californian roads and learned quickly that 28mm road bike tires with barely any tread were not well suited for gravelly, muddy country roads.

We took a quick standing snack break when we passed Gatwick, the halfway point. We counted maybe five or six planes pass above our heads while I realized granola raspberry cookies were in fact the most delicious food in the world.

Hills were mostly okay. Though what would’ve been 90% cardio turned into 90% strength training when I climbed brief inclines; each pedal forward up a hill on my single-speed felt like a pistol squat. Albert pushed me sometimes when he saw that I was struggling. I could hear his breathing go from steady to quick panting in the span of 5 seconds while I enjoyed the fruits of his labor. At some point he started pushing my butt instead of my back, and I decided it was okay (we were just friends) because the boost was more substantial when applied closer to the center of mass (the butt).

And then, we finally arrived.

So this next part describes the exact moment I fell for Albert. Let me set the scene.

We are looking at the row of houses with numbers on their doors and arrive at the one we are looking for. We get off our bikes. “We made it!” I say, and I open my arms, and Albert hugs me and lifts me up a little bit. It is half past four in the afternoon and we are both starving, very low on blood sugar. We bow our heads together over my phone, reading the Airbnb host’s instructions. I can tell that my brain is running low on fuel, because I cannot parse the 2-sentence instruction from the host. I read it five times over, trying to understand. Eventually we find the hidden key and get ourselves in. Albert goes into the shower, and I decide to make tea. I put the kettle on the stove and retreat into the bedroom to change. I smell something funky. I step back into the kitchen. The kettle is engulfed in dancing red flames, and it’s melting, globs of black goo pouring onto the stove like lava. Realization dawns: it’s an electric kettle that only looks like an old timey kettle for aesthetic reasons.

I am ashamed to admit that my emergency response was to try to break into the bathroom. When that didn’t work out, I banged on the door with both fists and yelled at Albert to come out. Then I ran to the window and tried to open it. I am again ashamed to admit that I could not figure out how to open the window. Albert emerged from the bathroom, shrouded by steam and dripping, a towel wrapped around his waist. It was the first time I saw him topless, and I noted with passing fascination the immense coverage of downy salt-and-pepper fur across his chest and belly. The cancer-generator was spewing god-awful plastic smoke and Albert - he laughed. He walked over to the stove, picked up the kettle, plopped it in the sink, and ran some water.

I think if Albert had turned around then and given me a kiss, I would’ve ravaged him.

He turned on the fan, laughed some more, and went back into the shower. Later, while I was taking a shower, he figured out how to open the windows too (the two latches needed to both point left).

We took a nice walk out to catch the last of the daylight and grab dinner and I had to fight the urge to hold Albert’s hand.

The ravaging came later. We watched the season 2 finale for House of Cards and discussed the episode. We did the mundane stuff like brushing teeth and washing faces, and then climbed onto the shared bed. A leg massage turned into a butt massage, then a nude massage. I took off my mouth guard (for very important protection against griding teeth), placed it on the window sill, and then kissed Albert.

The next morning was slightly awkward. We stayed in bed for a little bit, looking at our phone on respective sides of the bed, and headed out for brunch. The listing we found had a bad GPS coordinate, and we were led to a sketchy part of Brighton that had a lot of poor-quality tagging on dirty walls smelling like piss.
