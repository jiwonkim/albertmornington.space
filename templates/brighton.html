Tailing Albert to Brighton:
a tale of sore butts, lust, and 4 flat tires

The last time I had ridden my beautiful fixie before the Brighton weekend was in 2013, when I was briefly experimenting with a bicycle-train-walk hybrid commute from Mountain View to San Francisco. One evening I got off the train after a long day, unlocked my bike from the rack, and hopped on. I realized the brakes were no longer working at (or rather, while speeding past) the first stop sign. Further inspection revealed that someone had intentionally cut my brake cables. No matter how much I puzzled over it, I couldn’t think of a way for a perfectly solid metal cable to accidentally end up with a surgical incision.

From 2013 to 2015, I let my then-boyfriend-and-roommate use my bike, since he had no qualms riding a bike without brakes (he argued that it did have brakes, it was a fixie after - you just had to fight the pedals to slow down). Besides, his workplace was 0.9 miles from home (just enough to qualify for the living-close-to-work rent subsidy) so he wouldn’t be covering much distance anyway.

When I moved to London, my company offered to ship a big shipping-container-ful of stuff over. I fitted my bike with a rear brake and shipped it over, just in case. From October 2015 to February 2016 it lay neglected in my back yard, until Albert came over to fix it up for our big trip. We (read: he) fitted front and back fenders and plumped my wheels while we (read: I) watched three epidoes of House of Cards projected onto the wall.
