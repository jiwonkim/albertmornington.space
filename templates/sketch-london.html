Sketch afternoon tea and
(more importantly)
egg pod toilet stalls

I’d told myself I would do something very British for my 1-year Londonversary, but I hadn’t gotten around to it. So when Michelle invited me to afternoon tea, I decided to tag along. The whole affair was a lot more involved than I thought it would be. “Let’s just meet after lunch,” I suggested, and Michelle balked and told me that I should probably plan to skip all other meals of the day if I decided to have afternoon tea that day.

She suggested a few different places: Langham, Claridge’s, and Sketch. I Yelped them all, and they all looked good, but after reading review after review about proper service and lovely tea and delightful cakes, I was fascinated by a litany of reviews mentioning the futuristic toilets at Sketch. “Sketch is a collection of fancy toilets conveniently located nearby a restaurant,” said Tim O. in his review. Another reviewer waxed poetic about Sketch’s “awesome individual egg shaped toilets with soothing bird chirps to sing you to poops.” After perusing through many many reviews, I, too, was stoked to experience “being in ‘cocoon: the movie.’”

Sketch is conveniently located in Soho, but there was some construction going on in the area and it took me a while to find it. It didn’t help that the entryway might comfortably fit into a C. S. Lewis installation, what with the heavy dark felt curtaining the mouth of the entrance, which then yawns into a surprisingly pleasant leafy corridor.

The toilet pods were indeed futuristic.

The next day at work, during lunch at the pub, I broached the subject of afternoon tea. It was a polite excuse to start yammering on about Sketch and trick people into looking at pictures of toilets before their food arrived - sort of like when you ask someone how their weekend went, not because you’re actually curious, but because you just want to start sharing stories about your weekend.

Well, Charlie shared some opinions about afternoon tea in London (Brown’s, Lanesborough, one of which is supposedly frequented by a lot of Russions, I forget which) and I eagerly interjected: “and Sketch - have you heard of Sketch?” Charlie paused, frowned, and said: “Ah, the tea room for children!” He is wrong, of course. Sketch is for adults, too. Adults who like futuristic cocoon toilets and infinitely refillable tarts.

Yes, Sketch servces food, too. Here is a gif of me playing with a pink marshmallow. It had the pleasant squishiness of a flaccid penis.

And below are pictured the famous refillable sandwiches and tarts. Do not be fooled - they bring you more food after you have finished the sandwiches and tarts, sush as scones and cakes. So pace yourself!
